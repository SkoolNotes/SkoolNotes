differentials

For a function \(y = f(x)\), \(dy\) and \(dx\) are diferentials and the relationship is \(dy = f'(x) dx\).

For a function written \(f(x) = \text{(something)}\), the differentials are \(df\) and \(dx\) and the relationship is the same: \(df = f'(x) dx\).
