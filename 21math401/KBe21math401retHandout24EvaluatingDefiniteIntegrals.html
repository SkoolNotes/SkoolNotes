\(\int_{0}^{1} r e^{\frac{r}{2}} dr\)

\[\begin{aligned} \int_{0}^{1} r e^{\frac{r}{2}} dx &\implies r 2e^{\frac{r}{2}} - \int 2e^{\frac{r}{2}}dr\\ &= 2r e^{\frac{r}{2}} - \int 2 e^{\frac{r}{2}}dr\\ &= 2r e^{\frac{r}{2}} - 4 e^{\frac{r}{2}}\\ &= 2r e^{\frac{r}{2}} - 4 e^{\frac{r}{2}}\\ &\implies 2e^{\frac{1}{2}} - 4e^{\frac{1}{2}} - (- 4)\\ &= 4 - 2e^{\frac{1}{2}} \end{aligned}\]
