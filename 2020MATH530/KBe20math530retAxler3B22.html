Problem

Suppose \(U\) and \(V\) are finite-dimensional vector spaces and \(S \in \mathcal L(V ,w)\) and \(T \in \mathcal L(U, V)\). Prove that \[\text{dim range }ST \leq \min\{\text{dim range }S, \text{dim range }T\}.\]

Proof

All vectors \(v \in \text{null }ST\) must have been nulled by \(T\) or \(S\), and therefore must be in \(\text{null T}\) or \(\text{range }T \cap \text{null }S\). Notationally, \[\text{null }ST = \text{null }T \cup \left(\text{range }T \cap \text{null }S\right)\] Because no vector can be in both \(\text{null }T\) and \(\text{range }T\), the dimension of the union is \[\text{dim null }ST = \text{dim null }T + \text{dim }\left(\text{range }T \cap \text{null }S\right)\] Because an intersection can only make the dimension of a set smaller, \(\text{dim }\left(\text{range }T \cap \text{null }S\right) \leq \text{dim null }S\) and \[\text{dim range }ST \leq \min\{\text{dim range }S, \text{dim range }T\}\]
