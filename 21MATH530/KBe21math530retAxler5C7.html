Exercise 7

Suppose \(T \in \mathcal{L} (V)\) has a diagonal matrix \(A\) with respect to some basis of \(V\) and that \(\lambda \in \mathbb{F}\). Prove that \(\lambda\) appears on the diagonal of \(A\) precisely \(\mathop{\mathrm{\text{dim}}}E(\lambda, T)\) times.

Proof

We will show that for each eigenvalue \(\lambda\), there are at least \(E(\lambda, T)\) occurrences of that eigenvalue and at most \(E(\lambda, T)\) occurrences.

Suppose \(\lambda\) appears on the diagonal \(m\) times.

Suppose again that \(\lambda\) is on the diagonal \(m\) times. Each of those occurrences corresponds to where the diagonal matrix sends a (linearly independent) basis eigenvector, which implies that the basis of \(V\) has at least \(m\) eigenvectors corresponding to \(\lambda\). These \(m\) eigenvectors can be extended to a basis of \(E(\lambda, T)\), implying that \(\dim E(\lambda, T) \geq m\).
