#+TITLE: Dot Product
* dot product                                                           :def:
  #+begin_quote
  For $x, y \in \RR ^n$, the /dot product/ of $x$ and $y$, denoted $x \cdot y$, is defined by

  \[\begin{aligned}
  x\cdot y = x_1 y_1 + \cdots + x_n y_n
  \end{aligned}\]

  where $x= x_1, \ldots, x_n$, $y = y_1, \ldots, y_n$
  #+end_quote
* to work with complex vectors, we define the [[file:KBrefInnerProduct.org][inner product]].
